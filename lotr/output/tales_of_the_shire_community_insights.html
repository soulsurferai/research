Tales of the Shire: Community Insights

January 2026

Approach

We analyzed 10,751 comments from 2,160 unique authors in r/TalesofTheShiregamers spanning July 2025 to January 2026. Natural language processing identified 234 comments containing explicit feature requests, which we categorized into six themes.

Summary

The most requested features are not new content but basic interactions with existing content. Players want to sit on furniture they've already placed, pet animals they already own, receive dinner invitations from NPCs they already know, and celebrate birthdays with gift-giving (a core Hobbit tradition in the source material). Six themes emerged, in order of frequency: furniture interaction, animal interaction, NPC relationships, events and celebrations, quality of life improvements, and world expansion. Only the last requires net-new content development.

What Players Want

1. Furniture and Home Interaction

Players would like to sit on furniture and interact with items in their hobbit holes. The game features detailed interior decorating, and players want to use what they've built.

score: 32
"The main thing missing for me is being able to sit on my furniture and look around my hobbit hole in 1st person."
score: 17
"I'd love to be able to 'use' a chair in my house to drink tea and read a book by the window while the rain fell."
score: 32

2. Animal Interaction

Players would like deeper relationships with their animals. The game includes chickens and other creatures, and players want to pet, name, and carry them.

3. NPC Relationships

Players would like more reciprocal social interactions. The game includes a meal-sharing system, and players want NPCs to invite them over rather than always being the host.

4. Events and Celebrations

Players would like birthday parties and seasonal events. Birthday gift-giving is central to Hobbit culture in the source material, and players see this as a natural fit.

"Birthday parties. Giving out birthday presents is a big part of Hobbit culture, and it would add a new set of activities and ways to build relationships in the game. Get a gift at one birthday, and regift it at yours (as the books say Hobbits often do)."
score: 60
"The biggest thing missing from this game: Birthday parties. Hobbits love Birthday parties, and giving gifts. How this was overlooked, I don't know."
score: 22

Furniture interaction requests increased 27% from launch to January 2026. Animal and event requests remained stable throughout. NPC relationship mentions declined 23%, possibly indicating player adaptation or attrition.

Recommendations

  1. Furniture interaction - Enable sitting animations on chairs, benches, and couches. High frequency, increasing over time.
  2. Animal interaction - Add pet/greet interactions for chickens and other animals.
  3. Social reciprocity - Implement NPC dinner invitations so players are guests, not only hosts.
  4. Birthday system - Add birthday events with gift-giving mechanics. Strong alignment with source material. Also an opportunity to capture player birth dates for personalized engagement and annual return hooks.
